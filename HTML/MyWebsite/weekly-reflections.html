Weekly Reflections

Reflections made after reading book,
"Visualization Design and Analysis: Abstractions, Principles, and Methods"

Chapter One

  • Many resource limitiations, but change blindness was the most striking. Change blindess was the phenomenon in which a person is too focused attending something else in their view that large changes are unnoticed
  • Different views of data can change the outcome (Anscombe's Quartet with different structures graphically, but identical statistics (mean, variance, correlation, linear regression)
  • Visualizations are supposed to augment human capabilities and not replace the human itself. Example given was the automatic system for stock selling. No point in creating visualization when computer will automate the process


  • How would you know if the visualization you created was the best selected solution? Because people have varying opinions of the best visualization solution
  • How should we make the visualization better when we cannot optimize (due to tradeoffs)?

Chapter Two

  • Four Levels of Design with their threats and validations
  • Domain, Abstraction, Visual Encoding, Algorithm are set in a cascading effect where an issue in domain may affect the latter
  • You are able to start from the top level and work downwards or start from inner levels and work upwards depending on the type of work (Problem-Driven vs. Technique-Driven)


  • What does the author mean by downstream vs. immediate validation?
  • For enhancements of existing visualization, are they considered technique-driven? If so, would it be safe to assume that the other parts can be validated by the existing visualization?

  • Data has many abstraction layers to it and transforming it to data that is useful for the task is up to the visualization designer
